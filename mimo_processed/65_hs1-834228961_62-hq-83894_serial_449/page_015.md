###  "'Flying saucer' pics on show"
> PLYMOUTH, N.H. (UPI) — Pictures of a "flying saucer" taken by a 16-year-old student at a local prep school have caused an international flurry. Brian Pollock of the Woodstock School, Mt. St. Mary, New Hampshire, is the photographer. He took the pictures of a silvery, saucer-shaped object with a "subtle glow" from his bedroom window. The pictures were taken on March 24, after a rash of sightings of unidentified flying objects in Michigan and Ohio. The pictures show a saucer-shaped object which was partially obscured by clouds. Then it dropped down to treetop level and disappeared into the clouds. — UPI

###  "Now those flying saucers come to Canadian skies"
> Those puzzling flying saucers that have invaded Canada last week. Three sightings were reported in Ontario Monday — at Sarnia, Kitchener and Waterloo, Ont. Two University of Toronto engineering students say they watched a white and red circular object fly across the sky from their rooftop observatory in Toronto early Monday. Andy Higlewood, Osh, said it appeared as a fuzzy, white, circular object he saw briefly while driving north on Highway 401 in Cambridge, Ont. "It changed from a fuzzy green to a dull yellow," he said. Then it appeared fuzzy green and white. No other details are available, police report.
> In Sarnia, 100 people watched the same object — officially an unidentified aerial phenomenon — hovering for two hours over the Kettle Point area. Lawrence Bresette, a storekeeper, said a football-shaped object came to him about 8 p.m. "I've never seen anything like this," Mr. Bresette said. He dismissed the idea it could have been swamp gas, supported by U.S. Air Force Counter-Intelligence agent J. Allen Hynek, the scientist responsible for similar lightings in the Ann Arbor area of Michigan. The rash of U.S. sightings continued Monday, with reports from Green Bay, Wis., and Columbia, Mo.
> A fluorescent green and white object was seen by a teacher at Green Bay.
