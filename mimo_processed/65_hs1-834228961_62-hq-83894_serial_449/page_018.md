### Eerie 1896 Sighting
**By JIM HAZELWOOD**
The so-called Unidentified Flying Objects (UFO's) seen Sunday in Michigan by at least 60 people recall a bizarres experience which happened in Oakland 70 years ago.

The date was Nov. 22, 1896, and it was then that many persons said they saw an "airship" flying over Oakland.

But this date was 10 years before the Wright brothers made the first successful heavier-than-air flight at Kitty Hawk. Aerial balloons, however, had been invented.

The Oakland "airship" was the talk of the town for days and a number of other sightings were reported, some from as far away as Red Bluff.

Although the original story said the craft resembled a huge cigar, many witnesses described it as egg-shaped with lights at both ends.

It should be borne in mind that thoughts of spacecraft and estimates of airspeed were still sciences of the future when the following story, reproduced in part, was written.

> "That a huge airship has been hovering over Oakland for the last few nights has in the minds of many been conclusively proved. There is no person whose integrity is unquestioned, particularly who has seen the strange navigator of the air and this number has been very large. The excitement and skepticism has been pronounced.
>
> "Last evening at about 7:30 o'clock, the passengers in an Alpine Limited near Alameda caught sight of a brilliant stream of light high in the heavens off in the direction of Hayward.
>
> "They immediately saw the outline of an airship and watched its maneuvers high in the sky. The ship resembles a huge bird. From the rear end a stream of light seemed to rise and fall in its course. A light streamed from the nose of the ship, throwing a white stream of light for several hundred yards.
>
> "As the outlines of the airship were plainly seen, the passengers in the street car below became greatly excited.
>
> "The phenomenon had first been noted by a man who had been idly watching the heavens.
>
> As soon as he perceived the light, he attracted the attention of the other passengers and all, intensely interested, watched the progress of the strange phenomenon as it made its way through the skies.
>
> "It was high in the heavens. It appeared to be of huge size. It is thought that this was a craft floating over San Leandro. It moved rapidly, going at least 20 miles an hour. It shot across the heavens in the direction of Hayward. It turned quickly and disappeared in the direction of Hay-ward.
>
> "Not only was the airship seen by the passengers, but many other residents of this city distinctly saw the brilliant lights and some observed a large, bird-like body floating in mid-air.
>
> "None of the spectators were acquainted with each other and yet the stories were strikingly similar, agreeing as to time, direction of the airship, and description of the lights. These facts leave little doubt in the minds of many people that a successful airship has been invented and is navigating the heavens."

## 87 Coeds Observe 'Object'
HILLSDALE, Mich. (UPI) — A county civil defense director and 87 girls at an college today watched an eerie, hovering flying object settle in a swampy hollow near a college dormitory for girls.

William Van Horn, 41, Hillsdale county civil defense director for 10 years, said he watched the unidentified object through binoculars for nearly three hours.

It was the second straight night a large number of witnesses reported seeing four unidentified glowing objects in Southern Michigan. Sunday night a dozen policemen and at least 40 other persons watched the brilliant objects, which some described as four sister ships, land in a swamp about 45 miles northeast of here near Ann Arbor, Mich.

SCIENTIFIC CONSULTANT
Van Horn, who has been coordinating the sightings, said he called in Dr. J. Allen Hynek, chairman of the Dearborn Observatory at Northwestern University and scientific consultant to the Air Force's UFO study program, to investigate the rash of sightings.

Hynek will work from Selfridge Air Force Base at Mount Clemens, Mich., the Air Force said.

Van Horn said he joined the 87 Hillsdale college co-eds and their housemother to watch the object. He said it was hovering orange, red and white lights and appeared to hover just above the swamp some 1,500 feet from the dormitory.

It was still there when he left about 1:30 a.m. today, he said.

"It was just like a kind of vehicle," Van Horn said. "As I watched it changed from orange to red, perhaps, with a rotating motion, and had a white light at one end.

From all appearances it did not appear to be sitting on the ground as it moved back and forth across the ground," he said.

Van Horn said he saw three similar objects in the air Friday night. He said William Vinson, chairman of the science club of some 40 youths, reported several UFO sightings in this area during the past 10 days.

Van Horn said he could not establish the object's shape.

"Through the glasses it was just like a large star," he said.

"I think that what makes the light on these things change colors is that they are rotating in a circular motion."

Van Horn said the girls at the dormitory. The coeds said the object alternately glowed red, white, blue and other colors.

Then it rose up and and moved, stopping it ascent and sink again just when it was about to move into the glow from a nearby airport.

Its vertical and horizontal movements were too fast for any airplane, the girls said.

Hynek's role is scientific consultant to the Air Force's "Project Blue Book," the official title of the Air Force program to collect and study reports of unidentified flying objects.

Authorities and crowds of curious residents braved thunderstorms to drive to the swampy area near Ann Arbor where the object was seen Sunday night.

Washtenaw County Sheriff Douglas J. Harvey said deputies spent most of the night searching and checking out reports from people who thought they spotted the object.

The investigation began when at least 12 policemen and other persons said they watched the object in the sky over the swamp late Sunday night.

The sighting was only the most recent of several in Southeastern Michigan and other parts of the nation during the past few weeks.

The eyewitnesses accounts Sunday night included a man and woman who came to within 500 yards of the mysterious object which hovered near the ground then shot away at their approach.

## ENCOUNTER WITH A SPACE SHIP
**Binghamton, N. Y., October 24, 1965 THE SUNDAY PRESS**

### 'I Know What I Saw'; Egg-Shaped Craft
**By KEITH GEORGE**
Newark Valley—Gary T. Wilcox is a young man dogged by rumors.

People say his dairy farm has gone bust because nothing will grow on the land.

Others who meet him on the street express surprise, saying they'd heard he was in a New York City hospital being treated for radiation burns.

The handsome, 28-year-old farmer says none of these tales are true. The strangest of all, and the basis for the rumors, is his encounter with a space ship complete with its eerie occupants 17 months ago.

That happened, Mr. Wilcox says.

"I know what I saw. I even talked with them and even joking with them."

Mr. Wilcox touched the metallic, egg-shaped craft and heard its strange speech — perfect but stilted, smooth English he said, from beings who, when he offered to get them some of his farm's fertilizer, they requested, the ship lifted off the ground and was out of sight in seconds.

In the weeks after he reported the incident to the Tioga County Sheriff's Department, several people visited his farm by the dozen.

They include a woman who said she was from the government and others who didn't say who they were from.

But Mr. Wilcox, who professes he only wants to mind his own business and make a decent living, steered clear of such visitors. He had the telephone removed from his farmhouse after it was ringing so often he couldn't keep up with the chores, he said.

If any responsible agency ever determined exactly what happened in that remote corner of the town last April, he has yet to hear about it.

Mr. Wilcox did not seem particularly happy to see a reporter show up at his farm at 9 a.m. last week. He wasn't rude, either.

Further analysis of these data show that flying saucer sightings do not necessarily a phenomenon of certain population groups. Similar proportions of believers and non-believers are found among those who have gone beyond grade school and college. In weighing what evidence there is on the plausibility of flying saucers it is important to note that while most of the reported sightings have been explained as balloons, swamp gases, planets, etc., more Americans think they are "real" than believe they are "imaginary." Persons with college training (58 per cent of the college population) hold this opinion as do persons with a high school background or less.
