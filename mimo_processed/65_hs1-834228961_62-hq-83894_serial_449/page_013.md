## Would You Believe—'Saucer' Visits Wichita?

BY FRAN KENTLING
Beacon Staff Writer

An 11-year-old Wichita boy has joined the ranks of the thousands of people who have reported seeing "flying saucers" during the past week.

Johnny Sparks said that early Friday morning he saw two "flying saucers" that made a humming noise and were bigger than cars. He is the son of Mr. and Mrs. Calvin Coolidge Thomas, 916 N. Clifton.

Here is the story the way he told it:

"I had been upstairs to my mother's room and then I heard this funny noise. It sounded like two motors going top. I started to go back to bed when I heard it. Then I looked out the window and I saw it."

"There was this flying object that people have been talking about. It had colored lights and was spinning.

"One was in a car and the colors were purple and red and orange and green."

"When I SAW them at first, it was up in the sky. Then I saw them laying right by my window. I couldn't tell if it had any windows and it landed like an airplane. When it started landing it had legs and put them down."

"The top part spun, but the ring part didn't. It was like a big fat doughnut. The top looked like a cap that kept going round and round."

"The second one looked just like the first one. The first one came up. The other one just sat and spun."

"I was too scared to wake anyone up."

*When it started leaving I covered up my head.*

He said his mother said it might be the Air Force. None of the neighbors reported anything unusual when told of the saucers. Phil Smith, 3004 S. Clifton, said they had had no reports.

HOWEVER, JOHNNY and his mother did point out something unusual. Along with the concrete walk that once served as a reinforcement sheet along the house's foundation, a hole about three feet deep has been dug. It was noticed late Friday. Pieces were scattered in the driveway.

*Also, sand had swirled into the driveway where the saucers were.*

Johnny said he was not anxious to repeat his sighting experience. But, he said, "if they come back if somebody was in one... they would be friendly."

**WHEN CONTACTED** at the home, Mrs. Thomas said that about 5 a.m. Friday Johnny came running up to her room.

"He was crying that he had seen two flying saucers," she recalled. "He said he saw them come down and afterwards, she had been dreaming of flying saucers and possibly heard the humming noise in a dream.

She said Johnny had told her that at least two other people had seen them.

"I wouldn't know about the saucer activity in Michigan last Sunday because I can't see very well at nighttime vision. The Michigan report was attributed to swamp gases by an Air Force investigation."

JOHNNY SAID HIS saucers landed in a driveway on the north side of the house--nowhere near a swamp.

He said he saw a fellow in a fitted night because of an earache that kept him out of school Friday.

When his mother said he could have been having a bad dream, Johnny replied: "I had just fed my pet rat. It had 11 babies. I got back into bed, but I didn't have time to cover up before I saw them."

The boy estimated that each saucer was 25 feet in diameter. His size estimates did not vary with each repeated telling.

**MRS. THOMAS ADMITTED** she was a little embarrassed about the sighting, and she "couldn't understand a thing."

Johnny said he never thought there was anything like flying saucers "until I saw them."

## Object In Sky Near Bloomdale

Latest 'Flying Sancer' Report

BLOOMINGDALE—Two boys from near Bloomingdale reported seeing a "weirdly decorated flying object" north of this village.

Charles Cozens, 17, a senior in Van Buren high school and James Melvin, also 17, said they saw the object while driving in a '56 Dodge along the Allegheny road about 9 p.m. in the vicinity of the Cattaraugus county airport, a half-mile north of Bloomingdale.

Mrs. Ruth Kunkle, who was driving, to stop the car.

The boys said they observed an object off to their right over swamp, hovering in the air. The object hovered about 100 to 200 yards from the boys, from 150 feet to 300 feet above the ground.

They said the object was about the length of a car with a flat bottom and a rounded top. It appeared to be silvery in color. There were three lights on the object, two red and one yellow.

They described the lights as being one red light on each end and one yellow light in the center. The red lights kept going off and on, and the one yellow light in the center of the object was off and on.

The boys observed the light pattern of flashing for about 20 minutes. Then it suddenly went straight up at terrific speed. They tried to follow the object, looking up, until they lost sight of it.

### 5 At Pierre Sight UFO

PIERRE (AP)—Five Pierre residents reported they viewed an unidentified flying object high in the western sky for a half hour in the city about midnight Thursday.

They said they caught sight of a "spinning, whirling object" that appeared for a time to be a star in the Big Dipper constellation in the northeastern sky, but that moved in a southerly direction.

The object, which was revolving, is too fast to be an orbiting satellite, the observers said, and it took about a half hour to move through an arc of 20 to 25 degrees, they said, and then they lost it from sight.

They said the light appeared to be at a very high altitude, much too high for an airplane, and made no noise that could be heard from their vantage point.
