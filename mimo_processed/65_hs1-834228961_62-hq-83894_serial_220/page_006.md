THE FIRST PHOTOGRAPHS OF A "FLYING SAUCER" TAKEN IN FURNAS AT AN
ALTITUDE OF 8000 FEET

(Station Weather Photographer)

In Furnas, Osvaldo Rhodes Jr., student at the National School of
Engineering, was one of the passengers of an airplane who have seen "with their
own eyes" the flying saucers or discs which have become the general topic of
conversations and have given rise to the most varied hypotheses. But at the
same time the young man managed to get the first to have photographed those
strange apparitions. He observed one of them and was able to take its
picture against the skies of the city of Furnas. Picture of the "flying"
saucer at an altitude of 8000 to 9000 feet above the streets of Furnas.
The saucer seems to be a double truncated cone, joined at its base which
moves by oscillating line, let, propulsion and which displaced itself by changing
position in order to use its motive force in the direction desired, for which
reason it can be seen under apparently different forms. 2. Conception of
Mr. Rhodes, Jr., concerning the real form of the strange mechanical objects
drawn by him. 3. Another aspect of the flying saucer. 4. The future civil
engineer, Mr. Osvaldo Rhodes Jr., sketched in his editorial offices after having
reported his observations on the flying disc and after having handed over
the pictures of the rare apparition which he had obtained.
