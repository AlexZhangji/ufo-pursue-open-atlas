## Office Memorandum • UNITED STATES GOVERNMENT

**TO:** Director, FBI
**FROM:** SAC, Memphis (62-0)
**DATE:** 3-5-52

**SUBJECT:** UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECTS; MARVELLE, Tenn.; INFORMATION CONCERNING

---

Lt. Commander MILDRED HUHN, U.S. Navy, temporarily residing at 1900 Greer Lane, Nashville, Tennessee, advised SAC CHARLES J. HOLLOWAY of the Marvelle Aircraft Agency on March 3, 1952, at approximately 2:15 p.m. of the following incident:

Lt. HUHN stated that at approximately 10:20 p.m. on March 13, 1952, while standing in the back yard of 1900 Greer Lake and looking toward the moon, which was then in the southwest section of the sky, he observed an object which appeared approximately 20 degrees above the horizon. Lt. HUHN described this object as being circular in shape, approximately one-half the size of the moon, deep bright blue in color, very vivid blue. He stated the object had a slight reddish tinge on the far end. The object appeared to be moving from the northeast to the southwest. He stated that the object was not in his vision more than three seconds. It made no sound. Lt. HUHN stated that at the time he observed this, there were no clouds in the sky, the stars were out, and the moon was full. Lt. HUHN stated that the only way he could describe it was that it appeared to be a very high powered spotlight on a cloud, but he did not believe this could have been the cause of that which he had seen because he had not seen any spotlight or any search lights there during the evening.

The above is being furnished for your information, and no action is contemplated by this office unless advised to the contrary.

04:HLA
