## F.B.I. INTER-OFFICE COMMUNICATION
**UNITED STATES GOVERNMENT**

**DATE** January 10, 1952

**TO** A. R. Melton
**FROM** E. A. Reynolds

**SUBJECT** FLYING DISKS - INTERNAL SECURITY

---

### PURPOSE

To recommend that Bureau acknowledge receipt of Air Force letter on December 4 regarding a "flying disk" in order to clear the files of duplicate referrals from the Washington Field Office.

### NARRATIVE

Air Force files reflect that on August 26, 1951, *Cpt. William T. Smith, USAF, c/o Lockheed Air Terminal, Burbank, California*, observed a "disk" from his car while driving in the vicinity of *Route 466, San Jose, California*, on July 9, 1951.

The Kansas City Office notified the Bureau on May 2, 1950, that a former employee of *American Airlines*, named *Joseph Pappas*, advised the Kansas City Office he had seen a "flying disk" on October 9, 1949, at *Route 66, two miles west of Tulsa, Oklahoma*.

On December 2, 1949, the Kansas City Office advised the Bureau that *Cpt. Edward J. Ruppelt, USAF, 4602nd Air Force Group, Wright-Patterson AFB*, had written the Kansas City Office regarding the "flying disk" seen by *Joseph Pappas*.

The Washington Field Office reported on May 2, 1950, that *Cpt. William T. Smith* had advised the Washington Field Office he had seen a "flying disk" on July 9, 1951, at *Route 466, San Jose, California*.

The Kansas City Office reported on January 2, 1952, that *Cpt. Edward J. Ruppelt* had advised the Kansas City Office he had seen a "flying disk" on January 7, 1952, at *Wright-Patterson AFB, Dayton, Ohio*.

### ACTION

*F. I. Reynolds* recommends that the Bureau acknowledge receipt of Air Force letter of December 4 regarding the "flying disk" in order to clear the files of duplicate referrals from the Washington Field Office.
