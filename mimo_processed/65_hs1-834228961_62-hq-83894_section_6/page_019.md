## BODY (Mirrored Text)

During the evening hours of October 2, 1950, two Federal Bureau of Investigation officers observed a peculiar phenomenon on Venus Boulevard near 25th Street when they saw a round object sitting six foot in circumference about five foot down to the ground.

The object had the appearance of a discus and landed in a field. It being so light it did not even depress the weeds in the field. The object was lavender in color, described by the officers as a very pale violet, non-iridescent, and evaporating within fifteen or twenty minutes after it landed. When spotted by two officers, the two Federal Bureau agents took off immediately leaving nothing but a red, flashing pulsation.

This incident was reported to this office by the SAC Office and for about two hours subsequently searched at the appropriate search area for the "Flying Saucer" or "Flying Saucer" premises.

Mr. LYNAM of the office was informally advised of the matter by the office and issued instructions that the matter should be referred to appropriate agencies for handling and investigation. Upon inquiry, it was learned that the Federal Bureau of the above mentioned matter was personally advised of the above and nothing more is known at this office.
