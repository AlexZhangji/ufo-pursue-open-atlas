### The Death of Captain Mantell

The most tragic report the nuclear physicists had to consider was that of Capt. Thomas F. Mantell, a veteran Air Force pilot. A circular object, metallic in appearance, was seen over a Kentucky Air Force base on Jan. 7, 1948. Three Air Force planes, piloted by Capt. Thomas F. Mantell, took off in hot pursuit. From their reports, it appeared that the air object was traveling at the rate of 360 miles an hour. Two of the pursuit planes turned back at 18,000 feet. Captain Mantell flew on alone. He radioed that he would go to 25,000 feet and abandon the search there. The fact that his planes was equipped with reserve oxygen supplies, and thus could not fly long at high altitudes.

He never came back again. His plane crashed, and the instruments found in the wreckage showed that it might have risen to 30,000 feet.

"Our studies show," said Dr. Liddel, "that Captain Mantell and the other pilots saw a plastic balloon of the Skyhook type. Captain Mantell could never have reached the height at which the balloons apparently were sailing.

Several reports have been received of "squadrons" of flying saucers. People have seen little black specks floating high in the sky. This is explained by Dr. Liddel as clusters of 20 to 30 balloons, a foot or two in diameter, which are sometimes used in place of the huge Skyhook.
