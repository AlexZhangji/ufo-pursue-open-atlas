## Special Agent in Charge
Federal Bureau of Investigation
Department of Justice
19th Street & Pennsylvania Avenue, N.W.
Washington, D.C.

*Date:* [December 16, 1949]

*To:* Director, FBI
*From:* [REDACTED — Agent Name] (SA)
*Subject:* Air Force "Flying Disc" Report

I am transmitting herewith a copy of a "restricted" letter dated [December 16, 1949] which was forwarded to me by Director General, USAF, Reference: 8-1, Air Force Forces, Hamilton Field, California, which letter is designed to acquaint certain Commanding Generals in the Army Air Forces, District F.I., Assistant Chief of Staff, Intelligence, Headquarters, Air Forces Command, Robert Field, New York, concerning "Cooperation of FBI with AFOI in investigations of 'Flying Disc' Incidents".

It is my understanding from recent telephone instructions that we are to meet the Air Force personnel in the investigation of flying disc incidents. However, it will be noted from the attached letter that it is Army intelligence that it was now known that the human would investigate- evidence of the so-called "discs" being found on the ground and necessarily not those which were observed only in flight. In other, the entire letter of the Army is respectfully called to your attention for this letter and in the last sentence therein which states, "The services of the FBI will be utilized in order to follow the observed Air Force of the East or Northwest down all any instances which cannot be traced out to be seen never, fallen, landed and vanished."

In the first place, the instructions issued by the Army Air Forces in this letter appear to limit the type of investigation which the group will be asked to handle and secondly it appears to me the wording of the last sentence in the second paragraph mentioned above is closed in an attempt to utilize FBI personnel to induce the Bureau will be asked to conduct investigations only in those cases which are not interesting and which are almost, in fact, wild goose.
