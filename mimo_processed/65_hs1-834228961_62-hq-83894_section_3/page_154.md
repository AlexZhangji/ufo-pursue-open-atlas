## UNITED STATES GOVERNMENT
*Memorandum*

DATE: September 15, 1957

TO: Director, FBI

FROM: SAC, Minneapolis

SUBJECT: FLYING DISC REPORT OR UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT — AERIAL PHENOMENON
AUGUST 6, 1957
MINNEAPOLIS

### National Airport & Foss. Fuel

MISS RAMONA MABELL, 2112 Hobart Street, Minneapolis, who is employed by the JOHN R. ODEAL HOSIERY COMPANY, 1260 Second Street, Minneapolis, advised as follows:

Between 10:30 and 10:45 p.m. on August 6, 1957, she was sitting on the steps of her home with CHARLES WEND. She was feeling north and observed a large white object traveling at a very fast rate of speed to the south. There was a buzzing sound, not too loud but plainly audible. Just after this time the object passed through the area. This was a very bright trail a half a block of smoke, which was engaged in color.

MISS CECILIA TIMM, 207 Alton Street, Minneapolis, and is employed by the FLEETLING & JUGAH COMPANY, INC., Room 103, P.T.C. Building, Minneapolis, stated she was sitting on the steps of MISS MABLI'S home on the evening of August 6, 1957, around 10:45 p.m. engaging in conversation with MISS MABLI, when MISS MABLI suddenly stopped in the middle of her conversation and appeared to be frightened. Since this was at first she was sitting in a position in which she was facing south. She did not see any object in flight. She first noticed MISS MABLI's change of expression, and she also heard a slight buzzing sound.

HOWARD SMITH, 1046 Vernon Street, Minneapolis, an insurance agent, who is a former pilot of B-24's in the army Air Force, advised as follows:

He was sitting on the steps of his home around 10:45 p.m. on August 6, 1957, with his wife and his neighbors, who at the time were sitting on their steps next door to the residence of SMITH. All of the above parties were facing south. SMITH noticed at this time a "whitish blue line", passing quickly through the area. It took about three to four seconds to go from horizon to southwest. Using his experience, he would guess as a pilot that the object was between 1000 and 5000 feet in the air and traveling at a speed of about 400 to 500 miles per hour. This object did not leave a visible trail, but seemed to glow the size and like a giant spark or a long cone of fire, which lasted for about two seconds. A hissing sound soon followed the passing of this object. This sound was moderate and not nearly as loud as the noise accompanying the passage of a jet plane.
