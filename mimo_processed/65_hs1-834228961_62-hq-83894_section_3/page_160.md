## UNITED STATES GOVERNMENT
## Memorandum

**DATE:** September 26, 1947
**TO:** The Director
**FROM:** D. M. Ladd
**SUBJECT:** Flying discs

### BODY

The Bureau was requested by the Air Force Intelligence Service to obtain the Army Air Forces in attempting to arrive at an examination of the above phenomenon. The Air Force indicated that the Allied Military Flying discs might have been made by individuals or companies for the purpose of causing mass hysteria in the United States over the fear of a secret Russian weapon. The Bureau agreed to assist in the investigation of the reported sightings, and the field was advised in secure Bulletin No. 32, Series 1947, dated July 30, 1947, that they should investigate each instance wherein was brought to their attention the sighting of a flying disc in order to ascertain whether or not it was a bona fide sighting, an hallucination, or a hoax. The results of the investigation conducted by the Bureau Field Offices in this matter have failed to reveal any indication ofulative individuals being involved in any of the reported sightings.

On September 19, 1947, a communication in the attached matter from the Support Agent in Charge at San Francisco, dated September 10, 1947, wherein is enclosed a "reputed" letter that was transmitted contemporaneously to the one on San Francisco by Intelligence Officer, Boston 1st Service Command, AAF Boston, Massachusetts, a copy of which is attached hereto. It is noted that this letter, which is dated September 3, 1947, is signed by "Lieutenant Colonel H. B. Smith," Assistant Chief of Staff, Intelligence, AAF Boston Command, Maximum Field, New York, and is addressed to the Commanding General of the various AAF Forces. This letter is entitled "Coordination of FBI with AAF Investigations of 'Flying Disc' Incidents."

This letter states in substance that it was the original intent of the AAF/28, Headquarters, Army Air Forces that the OGA AAF Forces would thereafter be responsible observer, the FBI would investigate incidental or so-called disclosures being found on the ground. Further, it indicates that the services of the field agents were eliminated in order to believe the reported AAF Forces out of becoming down all the many instances which turned out to be "weather balloons" and "secret army maneuvers."

### RECOMMENDATION

It is recommended that the procedures outlined by the Chief of AAF Intel - 2. It is also recommended that there would be full activity in this field and that the Bureau Field Offices conduct their investigations and to relay all information to the AAF Forces. It is proposed that a circular letter be sent to the Field Offices to this assistance.
