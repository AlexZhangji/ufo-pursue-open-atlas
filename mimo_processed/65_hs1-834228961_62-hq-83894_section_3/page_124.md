## UNITED STATES GOVERNMENT — Official Use Only

**Date:** August 20, 1947
**To:** Director FBI
**From:** SAC Dallas
**Subject:** FLYING DISCS

Refer telegram to the Bureau dated August 16, 1947.

Enclosed please find the newspaper account carried by the Twin Falls, Idaho "Times News" on August 16, together with a sheet of paper on which A. C. UIR attempted to paste his impressions of the instrument which he claims to have seen.

Concerning UIR's sketches, it may be noted that he believed the outer edge which he attempted to create in his drawing to have been about a foot through. UIRH Kincaid believed that the thing or exhaust flame which he and his son claim to have seen was about a foot through and extended at least to the back end of the device. The flame did not appear to taper off not to widen out toward the back.

WILLY and KIRTY UIRH stated that they could see a notch on the side of the device from which the flames were shooting, and that they could see daylight between the exhaust flame and the side of the device. The flames did not leave any smoke or odor.

The Hove boy thought that the "side view" sketch should show that the device was more sharply angled from bottom to top, while UIRH Kincaid thought that it was more streamlined and curved, UIRH said that the instrument more come to a pointed or rounded top.

In his narration, UIRH Kincaid said that he had seen it on Tuesday. During interview, he stated that it actually had been on Wednesday, August 13, 1947, when he and his son saw the convergence about 1:00 P.M.

URH explained that he had sent his boy to the river to get some eggs from his boat. When he thought they were overdue, he went outside his yard to look for them. He noticed them about 500 feet away looking in the sky and he glanced up to see what he called the "flying disc". He said he could only see it for a moment before it disappeared behind a hill which obstructed his view.

URH Foster stated that the convergence was about seven - eight feet in the air. UIRH resting in the depths of the Stanislaus River (canyon), which is about four hundred feet deep, there were about thirty feet above the ground, it came about three hundred feet below the rim of the canyon, and skimmed along the steep walls of the canyon on the north side of the river. Foster said it could be doubted if the object was possibly fifty feet wide and stated that he doubted it could have had a crew of more than fifteen men. He further said that it was nearer to being round "like a top".
