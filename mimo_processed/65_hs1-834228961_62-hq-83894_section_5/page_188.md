### *Subject:* [REVERSED TEXT: H.H. HAMMOND/Photographs, Los Vegas, Newhouse; Sources: INFORMATION CONCERNING]

> On June 29, 1930, the Fortville Times, a newspaper of general circulation published at Fortville, Kentucky, carried a two-column cut showing three frames of a movie camera of west coast origin, reported to be a moving picture of a flying saucer. The photographs, taken by A.H. (HIMARK), Times staff photographer, were taken, HIMARK said, on a 16 millimeter speed-load movie camera.
>
> The news article, accompanying the photographs, stated HIMARK took about 50 feet of film in which "the object flying object showed clearly." The photograph, according to the article, was at West and Stewart Avenue near his home, 2200 Norris, at 5:15 a.m. June 27, 1930, on his way to see some moving pictures of birds. The article relates that HIMARK "suddenly heard the roar of a big plane -- a twin-motored B-17 -- and glanced overhead. At first he thought it was a jet plane from he looked to the west of the plane, which was flying southwest toward Standiford Field -- and saw the large disk. It had a slight concussion and seemed to be lower than the plane."
>
> According to HIMARK, the object "disappeared for about ten seconds ... then it began to get smaller, finally vanishing into the west." The article notes that while the "source" appeared on all the film HIMARK took, the airplane was only out of the picture for about 10 seconds. HIMARK, according to the article, advised military officials at Fort Knox Field, from mounted military truck requisitioned at Standiford Field. A copy of the newspaper article and photographs are being forwarded to the Bureau as enclosed.
