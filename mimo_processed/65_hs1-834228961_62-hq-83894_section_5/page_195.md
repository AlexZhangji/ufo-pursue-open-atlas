## [Mirrored Body Text]
> On July 26, 1950, Mr. ROBERT M. NOVICK, Publisher, Midwest Times, 2925 East Chicago Avenue, Chicago, phone Randolf 3-1400, brought to this office the original letter and envelope attached, postmarked 14:35, 28 at Chicago, which letter is quoted below:
>
> "New Flying Saucer"
>
> Since we are on the brink of a third world conflict, the world is more air conscious than ever. Aviation in same should be largest in its forecasting days. Who talk goes on about the flying saucers or disks. The Saucer we speak about, is not a military saucer, and is not yet owned by any government. The Flying saucer WHICH was maneuvered around Chicago Airport is a large flat aluminum which was all glass windows. It has two large jet engines on both sides. It is radio controlled. It resembles a saucer very much when in flight. The wings do not to seen on a clear day. This is so it is a good difficult target for anti-aircraft gunners. The reason for the large flat disc shaped craft is to give the saucers long range for sonic bombing. The ship was designed by ONE GEORGE A. TROUTMAN, Main 6618, Aurora, Illinois, and USAF Project 1234. The ship was financed by HOWARD KOWKOW, 5059 Aviator's Est., and is now being tested by the pilot. Main 6600, Aurora, 60. It is of the Martin Bomber. The ship is only made for war trips. It has a range of 4000 miles, ceiling of 30,000 feet, and a speed of 750 miles per hour. So far only a few of these discs are in use, and they really are plated in radar-proof armor as they cannot be traced. There are some very sensitive tubes of high detection to locate them. One small steel mill in *[REDACTED]* is turning them out. All other materials, such as aluminum and copper are made in the same plant with wings for the P-51 fighter-bombers and B-29's.
