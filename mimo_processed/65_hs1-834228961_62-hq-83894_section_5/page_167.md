## TO: DIRECTOR, FBI
## FROM: SAC, WASHINGTON FIELD
## SUBJECT: FLYING DISCS
## INTERNAL SECURITY - X
## DATE: June 29, 1950

This is to advise the Bureau that on June 25, 1950, DOUGLAS HARRISON, 3317 Ammack Place, Telephone, Decatur, EL 2757, and who is employed at the Hollop Sand & Gravel Co., Contract Avenue, W.M., informed that at 9:25 AM, that date, while looking out of the window of his residence he saw what he believed to be a flying saucer. He described this object as resembling a cigar, the end of which was tilted down toward the earth and presenting a silver appearance. He estimated the height of this object as from 20 to 25 thousand feet, and stated the size at that distance appeared to be about four inches. HARRISON further stated the object appeared to be moving in an easterly direction. He advised that this is the first flying saucer he has ever seen.

The foregoing is being furnished for the information of the Bureau and no action is being taken by this office.
