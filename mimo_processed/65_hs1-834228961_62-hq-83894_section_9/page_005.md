## Office Memorandum
UNITED STATES GOVERNMENT

**TO:** Director, FBI
**FROM:** Legal Attaché, Havana, Cuba
**DATE:** November 20, 1957
**SUBJECT:** REPORTS OF FLYING DISCS SIGHTING MISCELLANEOUS

The following information is submitted as of interest to the Bureau in the event reports of flying discs are still desired.

The Havana daily newspaper "Diario de la Marina" November 12, 1957, carried a dispatch dated November 11, 1957, at 5:30 p.m., reporting that JOSE MARIA SOTO and CAMILO CUNAR, who had just arrived in that town from the Matahambre Mines in Pinar del Rio province, claimed that they had seen a "flying disc" in the shape of a man's hat hovering over the general area of Matahambre for several minutes. They claimed that this disc was larger than ordinary airplanes normally seen in that area and that it was completely silent in the flight. They said that it disappeared at a very high speed towards the sea. There was no further descriptive information contained in this newspaper article. It is noted that Matahambre is located approximately 30 miles to the west of Havana. No other reports corroborating the above were received and no inquiry had been made by this office.
