### HANDLING INSTRUCTIONS
This material contains information affecting the National Defense of the United States within the meaning of the Espionage Laws, Title 18, U.S.C., Sections 793 and 794, the transmission or revelation of which to any unauthorized person is prohibited by law.

### BODY OF DOCUMENT
> FBI - FBI
>
> I have discussed the above matter with Special Agent D.M. Ladd who is in charge of all investigation in this field. He advises that the Army feels that these photographs are not genuine, but that they are sufficient to cause considerable comment among the public. The Army feels that the only way to overcome this is to provide something to show that these photographs are fakes.
>
> Mr. Ladd stated that if they are genuine, it is his feeling that they do not constitute a threat to the national security. It is the FBI feeling that they are fakes.
>
> It is recommended that the Army be advised that the FBI will continue to keep in touch with the situation and that should any information be received concerning any real or genuine flying saucers, it will be given full consideration.

### VIEWS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
> It is the feeling of the Bureau that the matter is one that the Army should handle and that the FBI should not be involved except in the event that some definite evidence is received that there is some real danger to the national security.
>
> The Army has asked for the FBI's cooperation in the past in this matter and we have cooperated fully. I think it would be advisable to suggest to the Army that they provide us with some information as to what is being done. I do not believe that we should do anything in this matter except to continue to give it our full attention.
>
> It is the feeling of the Bureau that the matter should be handled in the same manner as any other investigation and that we should not take any special action at this time.
