## NATIONAL AERONAUTICS AND SPACE ADMINISTRATION
**MEMORANDUM**

**DATE:** MAY 25 1959
**TO:** Director, FBI
FROM: **Special Agent (SA-1389)**

**SUBJECT: NATIONAL PHENOMENA OFFICE - INFORMATION**

**Re: Brigadier General A-41-559.**

The advice of **JOHN B. STREET**, Security Officer in the residence of **HOUSTON, TEXAS**, the so-called national director of the **Aerial Phenomena Research Group**, in connection with an article concerning the group in 1957, **STREET** advised that he was previously employed as a **Filmor** for the **City of Scotts** and that the **FBI** is seeking information regarding unidentified flying objects. He advised that the **Aerial Phenomena Research Group** obtains a national and an international membership of about 350 members and that they operate a membership of **35.00** per year for the purpose of furthering the exchange of aeronautic information for both identified and for unidentified experiences of their organization. According to **STREET**, the organization is a non-profit one and was established primarily as a hobby for persons like himself who were interested in acquiring information regarding flying objects. **STREET** stated that the organization was centered in **Scotts** and to provide the names of other **Hobbies**, all of whom were residents of that city.

**Tucson District OID, Hoodier AR Force See**

In view of the existence of this group and has been furnishing all pertinent information in our files regarding it.

There is no unusual contacts or other attachments of the **U.S.G.H.** In the **City of Scotts, AR.** **HOUSTON**, residence of the address of **1600 Ponder** Street, **Scotts**, having something to do with the residence of **Scotts**, for the **Ponder Group** in **Scotts** is completely unorganized.

No information regarding the activities of the **Aerial Phenomena Research Group** has come to **PONDE**. **VN** of the **Scotts** Police Department since 1956.
