### RE: AIRCRAFT ACCIDENTS — EXPLANATION OF POLICY RE: REPORTING
(For your information)

Reference is made to Bureau letter to all offices dated April 16, 1959, File No. 62-HQ-83894, instructing not to contact the Army, Air Force, Navy, or Federal Aviation Agency (FAA) (10-1-59), and that reports were being compiled in the Bureau for forwarding to the proper governmental agency. In connection with the above dated April 16, 1959, Bureau letter, it should be pointed out that the Air Force, Civil Aeronautics Administration (CAA), and the Bureau have agreed in principle that the Air Force is interested only in reports of unidentified flying objects and that such reports should be investigated by the Air Force. Furthermore, the Air Force has stated that it has no interest in reports of unidentified flying objects; however, it has indicated that it does have an interest in reports of aircraft accidents or missing aircraft and that these reports should be forwarded to the proper Air Force agency.

Future cases of this nature will be coordinated through official Air Force channels, and those of an identified nature (i.e., not unidentified flying objects) will be turned over to the Civil Aeronautics Administration and those of an unidentified nature will be handled through official Air Force channels.

*[handwritten note]:* This explanation is being made to emphasize the importance of notifying the Air Force and the Civil Aeronautics Administration of all identified aircraft accidents and missing aircraft. No further explanation is necessary concerning our policy regarding flying saucers.
