> The following is a report from Thomas C. Bledsoe, 103 Cooper Street, Crowley, Louisiana.
>
> On Sunday, May 27, 1956, Bledsoe, his brother-in-law, called Mr. Fisher, ... [text obscured/faded] ... observed a bright orange light, traveling east at about 2000 feet in the sky from southwest to southeast. It did not appear to be a balloon as it did not seem to be drifting in the wind. He stated the object was solid, bright orange color, traveling from all directions rather than drifting in one direction as would more than likely be the behavior of a meteor or the moon and another time at sunrise as bright as the sunset then disappeared into a purple like color.
>
> He further describes it more specifically. He saw the object cross no moon and just drift it was traveling and crossed it in a path that seemed to travel about 300 miles per hour. It could have been two of his buddies in the screen porch at 21. He had observed this object and also saw orange lights. He said that it was about 10:00 flight from above southeast in azimuth but no sound at all. He had been an Air Force flyer.
