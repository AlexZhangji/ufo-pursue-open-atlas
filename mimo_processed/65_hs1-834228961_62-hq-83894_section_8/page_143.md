DIRECTOR, FBI
8/15/56

SAC, LOS ANGELES (100-2148) 

FBI WING MEMORANDUM
PRIORITY URGENT

The following is being transmitted to the Bureau for information in the event an inquiry should be received from the Air Force:

On 8/14/56, LT. DOUG SATUR and SGT. CHARLES M. KENWAY, Board Air Intelligence Service Squadron, Norton AFB, San Bernardino, California, called at this office, accompanied by Captain DONALD HOMER BOARD of the Air Information Service.

LT. SATUR stated he had received a teletype from his headquarters, reflecting that LT. KIRTENHOFF, Van Nuys, California, had reported that on 7/30/56, a large spherical vehicle had landed in the back yard, splitting a large tree in two. On 7/30/56, this vehicle returned and on this occasion some little green men emerged from the vehicle and invited KIRTENHOFF to ride in their ship. He supposedly went up with them for approximately 20 minutes.

LT. SATUR stated he had no additional information except that KIRTENHOFF was believed to be employed in some airplane technical specialty for the Collins Radio Processing System.

Captain BORDES stated he revealed that the incident reported by KIRTENHOFF was presented on a CBS television newscast about the time it occurred, and that during the broadcast, the split tree was shown, and an interview was had with KIRTENHOFF. According to Captain BORDES, the tenor of the comments by the CBS announcers was that he placed little credence in the story stating it was a publicity stunt put on by the Los Angeles Times for the new television network.

LT. SATUR stated he believed the incident to be security frolic, but, based on instructions from his headquarters, he must make some inquiry concerning such a story so as not to result in any possible embarrassment to Air Force interests in the event the FBI would conduct investigation.
