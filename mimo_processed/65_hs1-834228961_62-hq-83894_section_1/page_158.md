## Office Memorandum

*Image: Scanned page of a typed document, faint and mirrored from the reverse side, with three black punch holes at the top and a blue circular received stamp near the lower center.*

To: Director, FBI
FROM: SAC, Norfolk
SUBJECT: FLYING DISK
DATE: August 8, 1947

Re Edward Bufflein No. 42, dated July 30, 1947, wherein information is set forth pertaining to "flying disks."

> There is enclosed with this letter a newspaper clipping from the "Norfolk Ledger-Dispatoh," dated July 3, 1947. The photograph appearing on this clipping is alleged to represent a "flying disk" which was observed by BILLY TURKIN, a Norfolk school boy, who was successful in photographing the object with his small camera.
>
> BILLY was interviewed on August 8, 1947, by Special Agent (A) THOMAS J. CONNOR, at which time BILLY informed that the original negative was given by him to a Mr. BROWN of the Photo-Craft Service, who in turn furnished the negative to the International News Service. BILLY advised that he had an agreement with Mr. BROWN whereby the latter will share equally in any profits derived from the use of the negative by commercial firms or newspapers. As of August 8, 1947, BILLY has not received any remuneration for the use of this negative.
>
> He informed that he was sitting on the front porch of his apartment which is located on the third floor at 1016 East Street, Norfolk, Virginia, around noon time on July 3, 1947. He had read numerous newspaper articles pertaining to "flying disks" and decided to sit on his front porch in the hopes of seeing one and attempting to photograph it. On July 5, 1947 BILLY observed a large, black object moving rapidly through space proceeding from the southwest to a northeasterly direction. He said the black object was followed by two smaller objects which also proceeded in the same direction. BILLY explained that the objects were moving at a very fast speed which appeared to him to be much faster than the speed of an airplane, and further, that the objects appeared to be extremely high. He said that they were much higher than the average plane travel in the vicinity of Norfolk and appeared to be above the clouds, and further that he lost sight of the three objects. BILLY was unable to state to what type the black object resembled, but advised that it did not look like any known balloons. He indicated that he observed small white objects trailing through the air, but that definitely they were not tear gas balloons. He said that when he first observed the objects they were at such a great distance from him that it was not necessary that he raise his head in order to see them from his porch on the third floor of the apartment building. He immediately turned around to obtain his camera.
