AT 5:45 AM this date, SAC Hood telephonically contacted the Bureau and advised at approximately 11:20 PM, July 9, 1947, the office in Los Angeles had received information from the Resident Agent at Burbank that a "flying disc" had landed in or near Burbank and had been seen to dart into hills where it disappeared. Further, there had been the case of a light in some woods, this light either in Burbank or possibly in the city limits for Los Angeles, which Mr. Hood could not be certain. The Office in Los Angeles had called the President space at Burbank and told him he would hold the disc for air.

Coincident with the information received from Burbank, the Los Angeles Office received calls from newspapers regarding the information. The newsmen stated they had called the Army Air Force Intelligence who had stated "we are not interested." According to Mr. Hood, this comment had pleased the newsmen and they stated they intended to publish this matter and would be glad to see their resemblance. Mr. Hood stated first that he had refrained from making any statement to the press rather than to admit possession of an object and that it was being turned over to military authorities in the interest (1st NA). It later, Mr. Hood confirmed that he did not want any news in the papers and certainly not one to the effect that we were not interested.

As described by him, Mr. Hood stated that the object was an aluminum disc about 25 feet across, pointed with a streamline casing and having the color of a radio tube in the center of the disc. This object was supposedly found at midnight and would be turned over to military authorities (AF-2) in Los Angeles this morning.

This morning's proposed to place the Bureau on record on motion of interest and to have the same and the information would be transmitted to the Director.
