*Office Memorandum*

*UNITED STATES GOVERNMENT*

TO: MR. LAD
FROM: MR. HOSMER
SUBJECT: FLYING DISK

DATE: JULY 12, 1947

Reference is made to Mr. Hosmer's memorandum to you
dated July 11, in the above-captioned matter.

At 11:55 P.M., July 11, I took a phone call from
the resident house of the Naval, Marinel, Teller, Police Department.
Jones said that he had examined the "flying disk." He said it
had been made from a GULL oil skin and the top of a moccasin toe and
had been painted with aluminum paint. It had been somewhat battered
because the clients were still hot. He asked if it were a new
birthday, a different shirt, some glass and a burner.
The house is near having anyone out to look at it. I told him that
we were not, that we had instructed the matter to the Army, and
suggested that he call them.

*[handwritten note]:* H. L.

JUL 23 3 31 PM '47
INTERNAL SECURITY
