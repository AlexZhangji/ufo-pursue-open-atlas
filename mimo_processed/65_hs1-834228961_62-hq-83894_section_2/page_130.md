**Special Agent E. W. Reynolds of the Fiction Section, White Office**

asking the above situation expressed with Unknown Control Center of the Air Force Intelligence, expressed the possibility that Flying Discs were, in fact, a very highly classified experiment of the Army or Navy. Mr. Reynolds was very much disturbed when Colonel Constant stated not only that he agreed that this was a possibility, but confidently stated it was his personal opinion that such a thing was a probability. Colonel Constant indicated he was certain that the Air Force was not alone in this interest. Colonel Constant advised he was of the same opinion.

**Colonel Constant stated that, in his considered opinion, the following:**

He pointed out that when flying objects were reported over the "Big Seven" of the War Department Exercises (exercises involving four services conducting research and collect information in an effort to identify these sightings). Colonel Constant further stated that, in order to date, we have reported sightings of unknown origin over the United States, and the "Big Seven" appeared to be fully informed. He indicated this led him to believe that they knew about these objects to expose to exposure to more. Colonel Constant pointed out further that the objects in question have been seen by many individuals whose word is beyond question. It has been further pointed out that several of the individuals are reliable members of the community. It is stated that it is his conviction that those individuals saw something. It is stated above has led him to come to the conclusion that these objects were seen and the shortage of the government know all about.

**The Air Force pointed out to Colonel Constant that if it is a fact**

that the experiments are being conducted by the United States Government, then it does not appear reasonable to request the FBI to spend money and protect the conducting agencies with respect to this matter. Colonel Constant stated he agreed with this. He replied in this regard and indicated that it would be a great blessing to the Air Force, indicated that it later it could be brought to the attention to the Air Force in regard to the interest of the United States, as a great service in connection with these matters.

*Mr. Reynolds subsequently discussed this matter with Mr. Donovon, Assistant Director of the Intelligence Division of the Air [Force]. Colonel Constant stated that he had discussed the matter with General [REDACTED] who is now the Chief of the Intelligence Division General [REDACTED] was in full agreement with Colonel Constant. Colonel Constant stated that the matter was of extreme importance.*
