# OFFICE Memorandum — UNITED STATES GOVERNMENT

**TO:** 27--51
**FROM:** CAB SERIAL
**SUBJECT:** 855 PLACE
**DATE:** September 2, 1951

### September 20, 1951.

*The following text is transcribed from the faint, reversed typing on the page. The transcription has been corrected for readability.*

With reference to the above-described flying disk, Mr. R.H. TAYLOR, of the Twin Falls, Idaho, was the principal source of information, Mr. TAYLOR, who is the executive director of the Twin Falls Housing Authority, explained that he and his wife, HENRY C. STEWART, a neighbor, were sitting on their front porch at approximately 9:30 P. M. August 13, 1951. He said that Mr. STEWART suddenly stood up and pointed to the sky and further stated that he had seen an object traveling at a terrific rate of speed in a northwesterly direction. The object was out of sight before Mr. TAYLOR and Mr. STEWART could see anything.

While they were discussing this, Mr. STEWART again saw approximately five minutes later all three saw similar objects proceeding rapidly in the same direction in the form of a triangle. As the group of objects was disappearing, three of the objects on the left flank peeled off and proceeded in a northeasterly direction. The remaining objects appeared to close ranks and proceeded in a northerly direction.

From three to five minutes later these same three objects saw another group of three objects proceeding in the same direction, and again in the form of a triangle. From three to five minutes later all three observed another group in a triangular formation consisting of five or six objects. They were proceeding in the same direction.

A few minutes later the three persons saw a large group of objects headed at from thirty-five to fifty flying in a triangular formation in the same direction. Approximately twenty-five minutes after the large group of objects was observed, similar objects were noted coming back over the city in a southerly direction. These objects were generally in groups of three, five and seven and followed one another at approximately five minute later-

Mr. TAYLOR called Sergeant T. HODGE of the Twin Falls Police Department, a next door neighbor, who was then accompanied by E. J. NOWACKI and BRANT MOORE, also members of the Twin Falls Police Department. The three officers investigated a few minutes and observed a group of about three objects flying in a triangular pattern over the city in a southerly direction. None of the members of the police department did not continue watching. At about 12:30 AM RAY MURRAY in her drive noted also saw in his drive noted two objects similar to Mr. STEWART. HENRY STEWART saw her husband, who was also at the object.
