### CIVIL CHART SUPPLY
Authorized agents for the sale of aeronautical charts have been appointed at certain airports throughout the United States for the distribution of aeronautical charts. A list of these agents, with the dates of latest changes, is printed in the United States Government section of the Weekly Notice to Airmen. This information may also be obtained from the Director, U. S. Coast and Geodetic Survey, Washington 25, D. C.

Requests for charts may also be made to the Director, U. S. Coast and Geodetic Survey, Washington 25, D. C., or to authorized dealers.

A discount of 25 per cent from published prices is allowed on aeronautical and auxiliary charts amounting to $10 gross when made in one shipment to one address. Charts are not returnable.

### MILITARY CHART SUPPLY
(See AF Reg. 65-50, dated 28 Apr., 1944)
1. Aeronautical charts, maps and publications distributed by Aeronautical Chart Service are listed or listed in the current edition of the ACS Catalog. Publications not listed but desired and deemed necessary in the Catalog will be procured and furnished by Aeronautical Chart Service through special order, when sufficient identification is given as to name or number, scale, projection, and publisher if known.

2. Requisitioning.
a. Outside Continental United States:
(1) AAF activities are to submit requisitions to the supply facility designated by the Theater Commander.
(2) Theater supply facilities are to submit requisitions for replenishment of stocks of aeronautical charts and move through channels to Headquarters AAF, Aeronautical Chart Service.

b. Within Continental United States. Requisitions from AAF activities within Continental United States are to be submitted to appropriate Regional Store as shown on Aeronautical Chart Supply System geographic diagram in AAF Manual 65-10. Requisitions are to be submitted to the Theater Commander. AAF activities are to bear the signature or approval by indorsement of the Base or Station Operations Officer. Quantities of charts and maps requested are to be such as to assure the maintenance of a 60-day supply at all times but not to exceed a 60-day supply.

3. Salvage and Destruction.
a. Aeronautical charts rendered obsolete for air by aircraft pollution, when not practicable to correct for conformity with new edition, are to be salvaged if feasible, else destroyed. Disposition by salvage or destruction is to be made in accordance with provision of AR 35-6860.
