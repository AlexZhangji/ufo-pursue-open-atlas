## DOCUMENT CONTENT (REVERSED TEXT)

*Note: The main body of text on this page is printed in reverse (mirrored), likely the back of a carbon copy. The following transcription attempts to read the reversed text.*

Special Agent S. W. Reynolds of the Intelligence Section had discussed
with Assistant Colonel George Carroll of the Intelligence
Division of the Air Force, the possibility that the flying disc or
saucers are in fact a project of the Army Air Force. It should be noted
that Colonel General agreed with this possibility. At that time, Reynolds
pointed out to Colonel General the possible embarrassing position of the
Intelligence Division of the Air Force if it was subsequently ascertained
that this was the truth, especially since they had rejected the mast
statements of the public in this matters.

Colonel General advised Mr. Reynolds that he would telephone a
further answer, and he now advised that a letter was addressed by Major
General Nohland, Director of the Intelligence Division of the Air Force, to
the Research and Development Section of the Air Force.

There is attached hereto a letter addressed to the Director, dated
September 8, 1947, from Brigadier General George F. Schulgen, which states
that a complete survey of research activities discloses that the Army Air Force
have no project with characteristics similar to those which have been associated
with the flying discs.
