## UNITED STATES GOVERNMENT
### Office Memorandum
**DATE:** 8-23-57
**TO:** DIRECTOR
**FROM:** SAC, PORTLAND
**SUBJECT:** UNIDENT. FLYING OBJECTS
**SECURITY FILE - X**

The following investigation was conducted at the request of the Air Force, Group No. 12, IQAF.

> VIA AIR MAIL, SPECIAL DELIVERY: 
> 
> Note 1: HQ 15, FOBBERT, DIXON, CREATOR
> Of the PBI, OREG AIRPORT, TRAVIS CREEK, ORE., advised (see PBI-312) at
> approximately 3:00 P.M., while instructing a student in a T-6 off at the airport,
> he noticed an object east of TRAVIS CREEK which appeared to be from 5,000 to
> 8,000 feet in the air. The sky was completely clear and visibility was
> excellent. He stated that the object appeared and disappeared at a simulated
> shooting. It took notice at it he had immediately taken over the controls of the
> plane which at that time was at 400 feet, and proceeded east in an attempt to
> further observe the object. He advised that the object appeared to be
> oscillating and traveling east at a high rate of speed without being affected on
> a computer in his plane at 1,000 miles per hour. He followed the object to the
> southeast and again noticed a darker object to the north of the first
> object. He observed no vapor trail nor did he hear any noise from the
> object. After scooting the area for approximately 15 minutes in attempts and
> student, HALLIN returned to the airport and saw another similar looking and
> take off. In approximately the same position at 400 foot altitude, both HALLIN
> and student saw the object at approximately the same position as seen before.
> He estimated the speed to be 50 miles per minute and stated when they first
> observed it if appeared to be so near he could fly right to it. However, the
> object started the second time appearing in the same manner as the first and
> faded.
> 
> It is noted that HALLIN served as a bombardier in the U.S. Navy
> Air Corps for about 32 years, flying constantly on the mission in submarine action.
> He is considered to be qualified and is reported to be a qualified
> pilot.
