We will find it heaven here.

We never will be happy here.
While this world's economy
Is based on hardship, strife and fear.
How do they live without a war
In their lands of milk and honey?
They don't need economy,
Because they don't use any money!
They cannot give us any ships,
So we must try and build our own.

Oh, my friends, we're not alone!

And vanish then without prediction?
Because they use free energy,
And there isn't any friction.
Brother have you seen their ships?
Sisters have you seen them glow,

**Not a Star -- Not an Aircraft, Says**
**Louis S. Smith Family of North Bend**

The Louis S. Smiths, who live on the CCC road, spent an exciting night from Thursday to Friday evening, April 7, when they used binoculars and a telescope to watch something in the sky which they were unable to identify except as an Unidentified Flying Object.

It was first seen by Phil, 15, a freshman at Mount Si, about 10 p.m. He was out walking the dog and the light in the sky attracted him were the bright lights. "I knew it couldn't be a star. Then I realized it was moving."

Phil ran in the house and called his parents and his sister, Diana, 18, a Mount Si senior. They all ran outside and all reported sighting the strange object.

Phil used a telescope (30 power) and a telescope binoculars and said he had a fairly good view of it. His father was unable to see through the telescope. "Every time I tried it the object moved where a tree blocked my vision." But he, Diana, and Mrs. Smith took turns with the binoculars.

"It appeared to be traveling from north to south," Phil said. "It hovered about 10 seconds idly and was heading in the general direction of Rattlesnake Ridge."

Phil described it as somewhat saucer-like in shape (see his sketch) and of a blue-grayish color in the moonlight. He said the band around the center was broken in spots and gave out the bright lights which first attracted Phil's attention.
