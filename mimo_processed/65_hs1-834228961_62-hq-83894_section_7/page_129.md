## Department of the Air Force
### Washington 25, D.C.

**Office of the Inspector General**
**Directorate of Security**
**Special Investigations**
**18 April 1952**

**MEMORANDUM FOR RECORD**

**SUBJECT:** Unidentified Aerial Object

1. On April 18, 1952, at approximately 10:00 p.m., the undersigned personnel were engaged in routine aerial reconnaissance operations in a B-29 aircraft, callsign "Korean Lady," over the Sea of Japan.

2. At 10:10 p.m., while on a heading of 270 degrees at an altitude of 10,000 feet, the co-pilot, *1st Lt. Joseph C. [REDACTED]*, observed a bright, greenish-blue light of unusual intensity approaching rapidly from the right side of the aircraft.

3. The object was observed for approximately 45 seconds as it closed the distance from an estimated 10 miles to less than 1 mile. The object then appeared to execute a sharp left turn, maintaining its high rate of speed, and disappeared from view to the north.

4. The object was estimated to be traveling at a minimum speed of 2,000 mph. The crew consisted of:
    *   Pilot: *Capt. John [REDACTED]*
    *   Co-pilot: *1st Lt. Joseph C. [REDACTED]*
    *   Navigator: *1st Lt. Richard [REDACTED]*
    *   Radar Observer: *Capt. William [REDACTED]*
    *   *Maj. [REDACTED]*
    *   *SSgt. [REDACTED]*

5. A request for an official investigation is being forwarded to the Commanding General, Far East Air Forces, for appropriate action.

*[signed: [REDACTED]]*
