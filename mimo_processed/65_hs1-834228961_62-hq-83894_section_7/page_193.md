## TO: Director, FBI
## FROM: SAC, Cleveland (1DD-0)
## DATE: 6-3-54
## SUBJECT: FLYING SAUCERS - CLEVELAND INFORMATION CONCERNING

***

On 5-28, Mr. THOMAS BLODGETT, 3712 Trapp Avenue, Cleveland 25, Ohio, and MR. KARL KLINKNER, 2429 Dunham Avenue, Cleveland 12, Ohio, called at this office. Mr. BLODGETT advised he is the operator of a Saucer station in Cleveland, Illinois, and Mr. KLINKNER advised he is the President of the Telescript Forecasting Company in Cleveland. Mr. BLODGETT did all the talking for the two men and he furnished the following information:

On 5-21 an ad appeared in the "Cleveland Plain Dealer" of a general circulation, Cleveland, Ohio, to the effect that on 5-21 a program would be presented at Tappan Field, Cleveland, Ohio, on "the Real Flying Saucer Story." The advertisement when Mr. BLODGETT exhibited disclosed no sponsor for this program and it was only noted on the ad that tickets for the program sold for $2.00 per person and would be on sale at the Central Ticket Office in Cleveland.

Mr. BLODGETT advised that although he belongs to no organization and is not interested in flying saucers, he has been interested in this tale subject and has been interested to the extent that he desired to know the sponsorship of the program. He stated that he found out from the Central Ticket Office that the ad had been placed by HARRY MAYAK, 536 East Watson Avenue, Terre Haute 20, Indiana. He advised that he telephonically contacted MAYAK on 5-22 and MAYAK stated that he was only the agent for the event and that he was acting on behalf of HARRY BROTHERS and CHIC PETERSON, WILLIAMSON, West Virginia. He stated that on 6-11 MAYAK called him back and told him that there had been a disagreement between HARRY, BROTHERS and CHIC PETERSON over MAYAK's assurances for the sale of tickets for the program and the renting of the barn.

Mr. BLODGETT advised that he told MAYAK prior to this notice to disclose either BROTHERS or PETERSON to MILITARY INTELLIGENCE and that their disagreement only concerned the placing of an ad in the newspaper prior to having the tickets for the program available in Cleveland.
