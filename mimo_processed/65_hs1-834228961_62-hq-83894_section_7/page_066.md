## MR 1246
In the care of Mr. Betty

### Dear Mr. Betty,

Excuse me please if I misspelled your name, but I never saw it in writing. 
On May 24, 1951 P.M. I had a visit in the 13, I, office, where I told you details on a certain subject you will frankly remember. At the end of the conversation I promised not to talk about it until I had received word from you whether or not the authorities were interested. If it was taken or not the answer might be given a few months else before the answer could be given.
Five months have passed by since then, and the subject has now become active in Houston, I am ready to agree with it. I have no choice but to check the theory on some reason and while doing so I found various compatibility as well as fundamental details.
I have now reached the point however where I would like to discuss the whole with an except and I would therefore certainly appreciate to know whether I have to consider our agreement or complete silence as still being valid and necessary or not.
It might be that you are too busy to write to me or that the authorities are not interested. As it is not my intention to bother you too much with it, I, I will take it that there is no longer any necessity for assurance on my part if I do not need any information to explain by the end of this month.
By this disposition I do not find it hard to force an issue but I would like to dispose of the circumstances that would place an obstacle to my promise to you.
With very kind regards,
