For the information of the Bureau, or upon further inquiry, the Bureau Division received a report from the Inspector General, indicating that on October 6th, at around 6:45 PM, a New York State Police Trooper, 69 miles north of New York City, 571 miles east of Rochester, NY, and 221 Broadway in Brooklyn, New York, observed and photographed an unidentified object. The object was observed to travel across the sky in a horizontal position, heading towards the 323.

> 1. ... 1. No attempt was made to determine their identity.
> If they did not report this object in an attempt to determine their identity.
> The object made two circles, followed by a rapid acceleration, and disappeared into the distance.
> A photograph of the object was taken, but there were no conclusive answers as to how it could have done so.

In view of the fact that there is no proof of the authenticity of the photographs, and considering that the Aerial Phenomena Office at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base has recently developed and expanded its operations, it is substantially forwarded to the FBI laboratory at Washington D.C. for analysis and examination.

However, it is not believed that the photographs were taken as a result of the action or inactivity of any person in the Division.

The letter is being returned to the local office.

It was further requested that in order to confirm the authenticity of the photographs, copies be made of the photographs. It was noted that the copies were to be made to disclose the facts without affecting the original photos. It was suggested that the copies be made of the photographs to disclose the facts without affecting the original photos.

In regard to the above, it should be noted that on October 9th, 1952, the New York State Police reported the matter to the local FBI office. A review of the FBI records shows that no previous investigations have been made by this office.
