## 6.3.1 Optimum Mode-5 Shaping Constants

The percentage of Delta vehicles that break up during simulated random-attitude turns are plotted against failure time in Figure 23. The same breakup qα’s used in the Atlas IIAS calculations were used here. It can be seen from the figure that over 50% of the vehicles break up, either immediately or eventually, if a turn begins between about 10 and 115 seconds.

### Figure 23. Delta-GEM Breakup Percentages

*Image: Line graph titled "Delta-GEM". The y-axis is labeled "Breakup Percent (%)" ranging from 0 to 100 in increments of 10. The x-axis is labeled "Failure Time (sec)" ranging from 0 to 280 in increments of 40. A legend indicates three curves based on "q-alpha in deg-lb/ft²":*
- Solid line: q-alpha = 5,000
- Dashed line: q-alpha = 10,000
- Dash-dot line: q-alpha = 20,000

*All three curves start near 100% breakup at 0 seconds failure time, remaining high until approximately 80 seconds, then sharply decline. The q-alpha = 5,000 curve maintains the highest percentage for the longest duration before dropping, while the q-alpha = 20,000 curve drops the earliest. All curves reach near 0% by 240 seconds.*
