## Federal Bureau of Investigation
## United States Department of Justice
## Privacy Act

June 8, 1949

Director, FBI

**RE: FLYING DISCS**

Dear Sir:

Reference Air Force letter to Bureau, dated September 9, 1947, quoted, **TERMS OF FLYING DISCS**, for the attention of Assistant Director D. M. LADD, stating result of an interview conducted by Special Agent, **JOHN B. BRUNET**, **MR.** , concerning the Air Force Intelligence, from **Headquarters**, **California**, and Special Agent **M.** . **MELVIN A. HURD** , with one **WILLIAM ALLEN HOUSE**, **438 W. Elm Street**, **Umbria, Atlanta**, concerning his observation and photographing of an object which was considered by the Air Force of significance from a security standpoint.

On June 9, 1949, **MR. LYN U. ALTHO**, **OSI representative**, **Umbria, Atlanta**, advised at this office, advising that he had been requested by his superior in **San Francisco** to obtain detailed information concerning this interview since **HURD** had requested the Air Force to return the photographs.

**HOUSE**, when questioned by this agency concerning the object which he stated his reflection was "hazy" on the matter. OSI representatives to secure the substance of information in the **Umbria** files concerning this matter. **MR. ALTHO** furnished the substance of the interview, as set forth in letter of reference and he has now recommended that he be furnished with a standard setting form this information in detail in order that he may make it available to the OSI in San Francisco.

A memorandum setting forth this information has been prepared by this office and is attached hereto. The opinion is recommended advice if such memorandum should be made available to OSI.
