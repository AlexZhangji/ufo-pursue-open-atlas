> **DEPARTMENT OF DEFENSE**
>
> **AIR FORCE**
>
> **"FLYING SAUCERS" OBSERVED OVER**
> **SAN DIEGO AREA**
>
> **INTEREST: SECURITY - X**
>
> These are being submitted herein two photographs of "saucers" which were seen at Oak Ridge, Tennessee, sometime during July or 1947. All of the information contained in this letter was received from MR. COLVIN O. D. CASSSE, Chief Investigator, Security Division, Atomic Energy Commission, and DEAN A. GASTRICH, Assistant General Counsel, Air Material Command, United States Army, who is the principal army representative at the nuclear reactor plant for the production of atomic research center at Oak Ridge, Tennessee.
>
> MR. RAINMAN advised that the attached photographs were taken by W. R. MUNDY in the early part of July of 1947. He stated that interviews with MR. MUNDY revealed that he had been snapping photographs of his family in front of his residence at 226 TALLAHASEE Avenue, Oak Ridge, Tennessee, late in the afternoon, when he looked into the sky and observed two silver disks as exhibited by the photographs numbered "1" and "2". MR. MUNDY stated that he took a snap of this item, was striking the film to the next number, when he looked into the sky and observed the item a second time in photographs. Upon viewing the unusual phenomena, he used his last film in taking photograph "3". MR. MUNDY made three photographs available to representatives of the Atomic Energy Commission and a copy cooperating with them was sent to that office. However, as shown by the photographic copy of the newspaper clipping, which follows, some published newspaper in Tennessee, the "saucer" was very visible in the evening sky did not receive any official attention; and it was reported by various observers at the time as a meteoric object.
>
> Upon review by MR. RAINMAN and Colonel GORMAN that MR. MUNDY had copies of this photographs and had distributed them among friends and neighbors in Oak Ridge, Tennessee. Upon Colonel GORMAN finding that the photographs had received wide distribution, he requested MR. RAINMAN as head of the Complaints and Inquiry Division, to recover as many as possible of the photographs, advise the persons in whose possession they were found to give no publicity to them, and to return said photographs to this office for transmittal to the United States Air Force Intelligence Service. MR. RAINMAN advised that he succeeded in rounding up twenty-five copies of these photographs, and that, according to the statements of MR. MUNDY, no more copies were made or distributed. Colonel GORMAN stated that GORMAN had supposedly exercised considerable overt activity in this matter and that the matter should be given no more publicity than was absolutely necessary. MR. RAINMAN also stated that the attached photographs which are being submitted herein are authentic and is submitted in accordance with the request of the U.S. DEPT. OF JUSTICE.
