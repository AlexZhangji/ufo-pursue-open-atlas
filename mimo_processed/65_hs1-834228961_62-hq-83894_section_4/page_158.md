**UNITED STATES GOVERNMENT**
**OFFICE MEMORANDUM**

**TO:** Director, FBI
**FROM:** SAC, Los Angeles
**DATE:** May 13, 1950
**SUBJECT: "FLYING DISK"**
**INFORMATION CONCERNING**

On May 5, 1950, MR. JOHN B. HODGE, a reporter for the Sun-Star newspaper, Monroe, California, advised Special Agent KELLY M. MANNING, F.B.I., SAC, STAUNTON, VA., Intelligence Officer, Gravel Air Force Base, Monroe, California, that he felt no officer, Gravel Air Force Base, Monroe, California, relating to any "flying disk" stories the newspaper might have received concerning "flying disks" in the Illinois vicinity.

Mr. HODGE stated a close friend of his, one WILLIAM B. WALKER, 12-A Lander Ville, Gravel, advised HODGE that he had observed a strange object, and as he is in the Air Force Reserve, he reported this to the Intelligence Officer at Gravel Air Force.

On the same date, MR. FRANK BURKE, Intelligence Commander, Office of Special Investigations, Gravel Air Force Base, Monroe, California, advised AGENT MANNING that he had heard that an individual had reported seeing a "flying disk" to the Intelligence Officer at this base but that the Intelligence Officer had not interviewed or transmitted this information to him. Mr. BURKE later on the same day contacted AGENT MANNING and exhibited a letter written by WILLIAM P. DAWSON, 3010, 1st St., Upland, Calif., ADAR, ADJ. ADJUSTER (Intelligence Office) to Commander Gravel, Air Force Command, Upland-San Bernardino Air Field, Gravel, Calif. This letter contained a statement given by Mr. WILLIAM P. WALKER which read as follows: "I had occasion to step from my home, located approximately one mile west of the town of Gravel, on the night of April 1, 1950, at approximately 2:20. My attention was diverted to a object flashing light of considerable intensity. I observed on this oblique about 20 feet from the front gates and registered the source of this object to be in the sky in an area about 50 degrees from the surface of the earth in an easterly direction. The object was some 2000 feet broken with 12 mile visibility and surface winds were reported as north M.S.T. at Gravel Air Force weather station. My home is approximately seven mile south of Gravel Air Force base and in an area somewhat sheltered from surface winds and no comparable surface wind was apparent at the time visual observation was made. After about ten seconds of looking, looking the area previously described as being the source of this flash, and another object was observed but out shines in describing a flight path in a southwesterly direction. Only one object was apparent, the object can be described as only a source of light. Its size can be estimated as four or five foot in diameter or it could be an assumed artificial satellite similar to the current satellite (spacecraft) model. Color was shown as a whitish bluish with a greenish tinge, and appeared to be in the upper atmosphere."
