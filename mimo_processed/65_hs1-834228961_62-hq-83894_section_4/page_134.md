### HARRISON, residence address Route 1, Box 261,

FOOT SMITH, "Thruston, is employed as a special delivery messenger by
the Fort Smith Post Office Department.

### HARRISON stated that at 5:25 p.m., Saturday, April 16, 1949,

while delivering special delivery mail in Fort Smith, he stopped his
automobile at a signal light located at the intersection of North
Seventh Street and Grand Avenue. He glanced up at the stop light and
observed a brilliant object moving in a southeastern direction. He
estimated this object was approximately 2 miles high. He got out of his
automobile and pointed up with the suggestion that other automobile
drivers, stopped for the signal light, could see this object. He do
not know who any of the other people are or what they saw. He was
obliged to move on after the light changed and the drivers began moving
towards their homes. He watched this object until it was obscured by the line
of trees in that section. He estimated he watched it travel some
3 or 4 miles before he lost sight of it. He stated the weather was then
clear, visibility was good, and there were no clouds near the object.
The object made no noise applicable to HARRISON. However, he commented
that he was stopped at an intersection where the traffic was heavy and
there was considerable noise. He first thought possibly he had seen
some type of a reflection on an airplane, but could not locate or hear
a plane anywhere.

### HARRISON drove to the 1200 Block of North "O" Street where

he observed at Army officer, wearing the insignia of a "lieutenant
colonel," standing on the curb of the opposite side. He stopped and
asked the officer if he had observed the flying object. The officer,
named unknown to the informant, answered negatively and then remarked,
"I am glad other people are also reporting seeing flying objects."
The officer then related that the day prior, Friday, April 15, 1949,
while the officer was driving from Fort Gibson, City bound for Fort
Smith, he also observed a flying object which he said was very similar to that
