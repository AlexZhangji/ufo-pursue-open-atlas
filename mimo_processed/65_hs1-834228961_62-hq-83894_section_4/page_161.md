## RECEIVED AND FILED U.S. DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE

### UNITED STATES GOVERNMENT
### OFFICE MEMORANDUM

**TO:** FILE
**FROM:** E. F. TOLE
**DATE:** MAY 20, 1949
**SUBJECT:** FLYING SAUCERS

### DISCUSSION:

Mr. Kennell forwarded to the Director a memorandum, which he had received from Robert Dippler which stated that he, Dippler, was the only subordinate flying saucer saucer ever recovered in this country. Mr. Kennell note on the memorandum, "To J. Edgar Hoover - Trunk".

### DETAILS:

This matter was discussed by Special Agent Kennell with the Translators Division of the Bureau, who advised that they had to determine concerning any flying saucer ever having been recovered in the United States.

This matter was discussed with Colonel William E. Garland, OCD-19, who advised on April 27, 1949 that he had increased interest in the flying saucer and related subjects and that in so far as could be determined by him through his sources in the Air Force, which are excellent, there is no substantial information available concerning the phenomenon of the flying saucer. He advised he would discuss with his subordinates at length to determine if any information is available concerning the recovery of a flying saucer.

Colonel Garland has now advised that there is no information in any arm of the Air Force to the effect that any flying saucer has ever been recovered in the United States. Colonel Garland is deeply interested in this subject and has instructed his key personnel that they are to keep their eyes peeled for flying saucers as individuals and to report anything which they know to him and which will not come to the usual channels from which possibly positive would receive about saucers.

The details of this case were not made by either Special Agent Kennell or Mr. Dippler with the Army or Air Force and but no notice of the telephone call was made to Wainwright.
