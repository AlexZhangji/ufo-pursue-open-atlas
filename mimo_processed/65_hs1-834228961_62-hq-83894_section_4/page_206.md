## Office Memorandum • United States Government

**TO:** Director, FBI
**FROM:** SAC, WFO (LPM: JME)
**SUBJECT:** MISCELLANEOUS INFORMATION CONCERNING FLYING DISCS AND SAUCERS

---

For the Bureau's information, several officials in the community of Alexandria, Louisiana, have recently reported seeing flying discs. The Alexandria daily paper has carried numerous articles concerning this.

At a recent meeting of the Young Men's Business Club in Alexandria, a member suggested that since Louisiana is known as a convention city, the club should try promoting a convention of flying saucer seekers. This pertinent comment appeared on the Alexandria paper and was picked up by the AP lines.

Mr. R. M. CASBECIO, a member of the Young Men's Business Club, advised Special Agent MARKHAM J. WARD that since the AP release, they have received a large number of requests regarding the convention of flying saucer seekers from many parts of the United States. Mr. CASBECIO states that the interest of the people is evidenced by their inquiries about convention particulars, stating they are flying saucer seekers and want to attend a convention.

This general further advised that in view of the volume of inquiries coming in, it appears that the APD will have to hire an office to handle all the correspondence. It might be noted that he advised a representative of the Air Force from Barksdale Field, Louisiana, has inquired as to plans of a flying saucer convention, stating that they are interested in furnishing an observer if such a convention were held.

This information is being furnished the Bureau, as it is possible such an enterprise may cause people to falsely advise that they have seen flying discs or flying saucers. Such falsifications might be made so that they might attend such a convention or become deluge tourist attraction.
